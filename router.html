What this router is

ASUS markets the GT-BE19000AI as the world's first AI gaming router: a tri-band Wi-Fi 7 router with a dedicated on-board AI processor (NPU) alongside the normal networking chip. It's the White version of the GT-BE98 Pro hardware, with an added AI core.

Headline features

📶
Tri-band Wi-Fi 7 (802.11be), ~19 Gbps aggregate, MLO, 320 MHz channels, 4096-QAM.
🧠
AI core / NPU (~7.9 TOPS) as a second independent system, with a built-in Docker engine for edge apps.
🎮
3-Level AI Game Acceleration — ASUS claims up to 34% lower latency across ~3,000 games; dedicated 10G gaming port.
🔌
Dual 10G + four 2.5G ports, dual-WAN, up to 20G link aggregation; covers up to 3,500 sq ft / 200+ devices.

Who it's for

High-end gaming setups, prosumer/SMB networks, and tech enthusiasts who want top Wi-Fi 7 throughput plus multi-gig wired. For an IT company it suits demanding clients, a high-spec office backbone, or resale to gaming/creator customers.

  • AiMesh whole-home mesh + wireless backhaul (via MLO)
  • Commercial-grade AiProtection security, VPN (WireGuard/OpenVPN/IPSec)
  • Guest Network Pro (up to 5 SSIDs), ROG Aura RGB

Sources: ASUS press release · ROG India article

Reviews & known issues

👍 What reviewers love

  • Dong Knows Tech: 8/10 overall — top-tier real-world Wi-Fi 7 (sustained >3 Gbps), ~8,500 Mbps wired on 10G fiber.
  • Dual 10G + four 2.5G ports with dual-WAN; runs cool and quiet; no vendor login required.

👎 Watch-outs

  • AI / Docker features are buggy and impractical right now ("extremely buggy" per the review).
  • Expensive (~$900 / "as much as a mini PC"); bulky, not wall-mountable.
  • Reviewer advises using the web interface, not the ASUS mobile app, for setup.
